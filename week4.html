    • Trust-less p2p payments framework using UPI 2.0 mandates

      Highlighted below is the framework we've designed. TL;DR : We use UPI mandates to create payment promises from driver to the customer, and customer to the driver. If one of them violates the contract (doesn't turn up, etc), then the other person gets the money. Otherwise it is nullified. We believe that aligning monetary incentives with future payment-promises will lead to the desired co-operative behaviour.


      Objectives

      This document attempts to detail a trust-less payments system built over UPI 2.0 Mandates for transport. Prerequisite information and current context has been added to aid readers.


      Current Customer-Driver interaction
      1. Customers can find Rickshaws parked in a queue near bus/railway stations, popular locations etc.
      2. They can also find Rickshaws by waving their hand at Rickshaws moving on the road to signal their intent to negotiate a journey.
      3. A customer who wants to travel approaches the first Rickshaw and starts negotiating with the driver.
      4. If negotiations fail, they move onto the next Rickshaw and resume their negotiations.
      5. The customer travels in the first Rickshaw with which negotiations are successful.

      Proposed Customer-Driver interaction
      1. The customer broadcasts their intention to go from place-A to place-B. They also signal the maximum amount they are willing to pay above the standard meter fare.
      2. The bid is seen by drivers near the customer. On the driver’s app, they see the fare slowly rise over one minute from the standard meter fare to the maximum amount the user is willing to pay.
      3. The first driver to accept the bid gets the bid and will arrive at the agreed upon pickup place to pick up the customer
      4. After dropping off the customer, the customer pays the agreed upon price, and the trip ends.

      Technical Flow
      1. The customer broadcasts a bid which is a message which contains the following information:
        • Pickup and destination location - from where the customer expects to be picked up, and where they expect to be dropped off.
        • Extra Fare - how much the customer is willing to pay above the meter fare.
        • Customer UPI ID - the UPI ID of the customer.
      2. The driver who’s bid is accepted responds to the customer with the extra fare that they are willing to accept, their UPI ID, and a mandate token that the customer can verify. The customer verifies the token:
        • If the customer finds that the token is invalid, they report it to the server who verifies the mandate and penalizes the driver if it is found that the token is invalid. Else they penalize the customer.
        • If the customer finds that the token is valid, they can accept the bid by responding to the driver with their own mandate.
      3. The driver then verifies the mandate sent by the customer:
        • If the driver finds that the token is invalid, they report it to the server who verifies the mandate and penalizes the customer accordingly. Else they penalize the driver.
        • If the driver finds that the token is valid, they can accept the bid and start the trip.
      4. Once the bid is agreed upon and verified by both parties, they are both required to arrive at the pickup location within 5 minutes. Both the customer’s and driver’s GPS are constantly streamed to the server. Either one can cancel the booking if the terms of the contract are violated.
      5. When one party detects the other party has violated the contract, they can revoke their own mandate and execute the other party’s mandate.
      6. If one party detects that their mandate has been executed unfairly while the other party has revoked their mandate, they can appeal to the server.
        • If the 5 minutes are not up, the other party is penalized.
        • If party-1 complains:
          • If there are anomalies in party-2’s GPS, party-2 is penalized.
          • If party-2 isn’t at the pickup location, party-2 is penalized.
          • If there are anomalies in party-1’s GPS, party-1 is penalized.
          • If party-1 isn’t at the pickup location, party-1 is penalized.
          • If both parties are at the pickup location, party-2 is penalized.
      7. If both parties are at the pickup location and they cannot find each other, they are free to revoke their own mandate if the 5 minutes are up.
      8. Once they both arrive, they can start the trip with a successful OTP verification. At that moment both parties cancel their mandates and the trip starts. Both parties see the fare to be paid on the app and can execute the transaction via UPI signed intent or cash.

      Verifying Mandate Revocation/Execution
      If either the customer or the driver feels that they were wronged on a mandate, they can prove to the server that a given mandate was not executed fairly by sharing with the server the parameters needed to verify a transaction which the server can then use to verify the result of that transaction and compare against the required value.

      Penalizing Invalid Mandate Revocation/Execution
      When penalizing a party for acting unfairly, the server can detect contract violations using the method above. If it detects there has been an unfair act committed, it penalizes the party in question in the following way: 1. It takes some amount from the security deposit of the cheating party and gives it to the aggrieved party. 2. It reduces the score of the cheating party which will reduce their chances of securing a ride in the future.

      GPS Anomaly Detection
      By constantly tracking the GPS of the customer and driver, we can detect anomalies by comparing their GPS data against historical GPS data.
