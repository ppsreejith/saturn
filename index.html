What is the meaning of this?

{Problem Statement / Motivation}

Traffic is a horrific problem in an otherwise amazing city like Bangalore (can be extrapolated to other fast-growing cities of the world). An average Bangalorean spends xmins everyday on the road, cursing his/her fate. Additionally, y of the 15 most polluted cities in the world are in India. We believe that adoption of public transport is key to solving the tyranny of traffic and to ease pressure on the environent. We were wondering why we ourselves don't use public transport and 3 key issues stood out - uncertainty around ETA, uncertainty around occupancy/comfort, uncertainty around last-mile connectivity. We think that if we eliminate all the 3 barriers and provide a seamless Uber-like experience, public transport becomes a no-brainer, given that it is already 5x-10x cheaper. People are also used to the seamless payment experience most ride-hailing services provide. We want to do something about this.

What do you propose?

{Solution / Philosophy / (Unnecessarily) Grand Vision}

Real-time Data Streams + Journey Planning + Online Payment
Imagine entering a destination on the Saturn app, similar to ride hailing apps. Saturn creates end-to-end piece-wise journeys for you, with ETA (from GPS data), occupancy index (from the Deep Learning Module or ticketing info.) and cost. You can choose a journey optimizing for time, money or your comfort. Saturn uses Buses and Metros for most of the journey and auto-rickshaws for inter-modal and first/last mile connectivity. As your bus ride is ending, Saturn automatically books an auto-rickshaw so that when you get off the bus, Payment at public transport is done through NFC readers at the door, or by scanning QR codes. Payment to auto-rickshaw drivers is done via UPI (P2P payment).

Meter Mélé
The first 2 bottlenecks can be solved with the above strategies. But if you've ever been to India, you know how hazardous talking to an angry auto-rickshaw driver can be. We don't think it's entirely their fault. Same fares during peak and off-peak times can be widely inefficient. So, they resort to driving up fares knowing fully well you cannot haggle with more than 2-3 auto-drivers. We need a Game-theoretically sound strategy to align incentives of all parties. What if you could scale haggling? Meter Mélé (translated from Kannada; means 'on top of the meter', a common way for drivers to ask for extra money on top of the fare) is that solution. Customers enter the maximum Meter Mélé amount they are willing to pay. This bid is broadcast to all nearby drivers starting from 0 to the maximum amount, with time. Drivers are incentivised to accept the fare that they consider is fair, especially considering other drivers will be doing exactly that. We think this will converge to 'true fares' in the market creating a win-win for everyone.

Government/ Activists' Dashboard
Currently activists and the govt. have unbelievably little data to optimize routes, increase infrastructure, etc. We think the data generated from Saturn will fill this void effectively. Apps can be built on top of data aggregations for officials and activists to consume.

FAQs

{What about these things, huh?}

1. How is this different from Google Maps?
Saturn is basically Google Maps Navigation on steroids. Google does not mix modes in journeys, Saturn will. You will also get real time ETA, which is not there in Google currently. Also, Google has no idea about occupancy which is vital to people adopting ore public transport. Saturn also has an incentive aligning feature : Meter mele, which we believe is a crucial part this system working. Saturn is different from Google Maps because it is much much much more than Google Maps.


2. How is this different from Uber/Ola?
Saturn will fundamentally be open. Saturn will pay P2P; no obfuscation from drivers. Also, Saturn will drive traffic towards public transport, and not away from it. We want Saturn to be very similar to the seamless Uber experience we're all used to, though :)


3. Isn't having a camera on a bus dangerous?
We're as concerned of the Big Brother govt as you are. The deep learning module is privacy first - the evaluation will happen on the device and no image is ever uploaded. The code will also be in public domain.


4. Can you both really complete this?
We think so. We're fairly quick in getting MVPs out. We've worked on technologies like Augmented Reality, IoT, blockchain, Deep Learning, chatbots, voicebots etc. apart from the usual suspects (Node, React, Python etc). We think we should be able to build most features except payments (that'll require interfacing with a bank and other legal shiz). Scheduling meetings with officials will be tricky, and we want to focus on this.
